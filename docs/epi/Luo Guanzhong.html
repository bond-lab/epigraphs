      EPIGRAPH: On and on the Great River rolls, racing east. Of proud and gallant heroes its white-tops leave no trace, As right and wrong, pride and fall turn all at once unreal. Yet ever the green hills stay To blaze in the west-wanting day. Fishers and woodsmen comb the river isles. White-crowned, they've seen enough of spring and autumn To make good company over the wine jar, Where many a famed event Provides their merriment.
