Above thy woods, Memphis, pyramids pale Peered as we passed; and Nile's soft azure hue, Gleaming 'mid the gray desert, met the view; Where hung at intervals the scarce seen sail. Oh! were this little boat to us the world, As thus we wandered far from sounds of care, Circled with friends, and gentle maidens fair, While southern airs the waving pennant curled, How sweet were life's long voyage, till in peace We gained that haven still, where all things cease!
